Life and Love—two things, just these two things.

There’s so much to talk about, but what’s the point? Like, does it even matter? We all live—for ourselves, for our future, for getting rich, for checking off our bucket lists, for traveling, for experiences, or just to figure out where we stand as humans. When I look at the stars at night, all I see are dots. Those dots—and honestly—they make me wonder: does anything even matter? Why am I writing all this at 4 AM? The truth is, it doesn’t. You’re born, you live, and you die.

Then there’s love—the strangest, weirdest thing in the world. What part of our nature, survival, or whatever higher force decided to throw this into our existence? One day, you’re just minding your own business, not caring about a thing in the world. And the next moment, you meet someone, and suddenly, you start seeing things that don’t even exist. You start seeing life. Until that moment, you were living life on autopilot, like muscle memory. But now, you actually see it—you see a future, you see possibilities, you see things that aren’t even there yet.

But not always. Sometimes, Love and Life don’t get along.

The whole point of all this? There is no point. You just do things, and you keep doing them until you die. So, if you’re doing something, put everything into it. Because you never know if it’s going to last forever or if it’s just another seesaw between Love and Life.

Just like Life and Love, the Brain and Heart come into the picture. I’m not going to explain the whole voodoo behind the Brain and Heart thing, but let me tell you—it hits hard. It slaps you in the face when you try to take control. And honestly, I don’t even know who’s asking for control. Is it my brain telling me to get a grip? Or my heart doing the opposite? I don’t know, and I don’t want to know. Because these feelings—yeah, these feelings—they’re proof that you exist. You exist real good. Whether it matters or not, you exist.

In the end, either Life will end you, or Love will resurrect you.
